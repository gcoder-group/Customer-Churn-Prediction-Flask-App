This page shows the performance metrics of the Customer Churn Prediction model. These metrics are calculated on the entire dataset to evaluate how well the model can predict customer churn.

F1 Score

--

Harmonic mean

Accuracy measures the overall correctness of the model.

Formula: (TP + TN) / Total Predictions

In simpler terms: Out of all predictions, how many were correct?

Example: If accuracy is 75%, it means the model correctly predicts churn 75 out of 100 times.

Precision measures how many of the predicted churners actually churned.

Formula: TP / (TP + FP)

This tells us: When the model says a customer will churn, how often is it correct?

Example: If precision is 60%, when the model predicts a customer will churn, it's correct 60% of the time.

Recall (also called Sensitivity) measures how many actual churners the model correctly identifies.

Formula: TP / (TP + FN)

This tells us: Out of all customers who actually churned, how many did we catch?

Example: If recall is 80%, the model catches 80% of all customers who will churn.

F1 Score is the harmonic mean of Precision and Recall. It balances both metrics.

Formula: 2 × (Precision × Recall) / (Precision + Recall)

Useful when you need a balance between catching churners (recall) and not wasting resources on false predictions (precision).

Example: A high F1 score means the model has both good precision and good recall.
